I have spent the last several years standing up enterprise OpenShift and Kubernetes at scale, and I am ready to bring that reliability rigor to model-training and inference infrastructure.

On the years-of-experience bar: I bring roughly 3.5 years of direct DevOps/SRE work plus a year of CTO-level infrastructure ownership, and I am currently a CS PhD candidate at George Mason's DCXR Lab researching generative AI and graphics pipelines, so the hybrid of hands-on platform craft and applied-AI research is the breadth I would bring to a research-adjacent SRE seat.

What pulls me to Mistral specifically is the open-weights philosophy -- it resonates with the open, reproducible posture I have practiced in academic AI research, and it is the kind of culture where SRE for model training is technical work that compounds rather than disappears behind a closed API. I would welcome a conversation about how I can contribute to the NYC infrastructure build-out.
