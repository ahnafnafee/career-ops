# Training Evaluation: Certified Kubernetes Administrator (CKA)

**Date:** 2026-04-27
**Provider:** Cloud Native Computing Foundation (CNCF) / Linux Foundation
**Cost:** $445 (often $300-360 with promotional codes / Linux Foundation memberships)
**Format:** Hands-on performance-based exam, 2 hours, ~15-20 tasks via shell on a remote K8s cluster
**Prerequisite:** None formally; in practice 6-12 months of working K8s exposure expected
**Verdict:** **DO IT**

## Why this is the right cert for you

- **Direct gap closer.** Three of your top picks flagged K8s/SRE depth as the load-bearing skill (#15 Deepgram SRE, #10 Clay Labs, #24 Mistral SRE). You already do this work — CKA formalizes it.
- **Evergreen at your seniority band.** Senior DevOps/SRE roles ($160-210K) treat CKA as the floor signal. CKS (security) is the rarer differentiator if you want to stack a second cert.
- **Recruiter ATS hits.** "CKA" / "Certified Kubernetes Administrator" appears in keyword scans at most AI-infra-heavy companies.

## 6-Week Plan

| Week | Focus | Hours |
|------|-------|-------|
| 1-2 | KodeKloud or Killer.sh interactive labs (cluster operations, workloads, scheduling) | 12-15 |
| 3-4 | kubernetes.io docs deep dive (RBAC, networking/CNI, storage, security contexts, etcd/control-plane) | 12-15 |
| 5 | 2-3 full-length practice exams on Killer.sh (their session is the closest to the real exam) | 8-10 |
| 6 | Schedule + take exam. Buffer for retake (free first retake included). | 4 |

**Total:** ~40-50 study hours over 6 weeks.

## Resources

- **Primary courses:** KodeKloud CKA (~$80, lifetime) OR Mumshad Mannambeth's Udemy course (~$15 on sale)
- **Lab sim:** Killer.sh (2 free sessions included with exam registration) — closest to actual exam UX
- **Docs to bookmark:** kubernetes.io (allowed during exam), `kubectl` cheatsheet
- **Free reference:** Cloud Native Computing Foundation curriculum repo on GitHub

## Risks to manage

- **Don't over-invest in flashcards.** The exam is hands-on; muscle memory matters more than recall.
- **Don't rely only on `kubectl run` shortcuts.** Some questions force you to write YAML from scratch under time pressure — practice that explicitly.
- **Free retake is included** — schedule confidently; if you fail, you have one re-do.
